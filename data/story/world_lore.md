# Into the Woods — World Lore Bible (v0.4)

Tone target: **retro Sierra adventure + ecological mystery + family intimacy**.

---

## 1) Regional Map of Play Space

### The Hearthline Cabin (Home Region)
- A small inherited timber cabin at the edge of the Brindlewood.
- Serves as emotional anchor: warmth, routine, safety, shared investigation board.
- Environmental state reflects family trust and story progression:
  - weak fire, cluttered table, uncertain mood at start
  - steadier light, organized clues, softer dialogue as trust grows

### Brindlewood Verge (Outer Forest)
- First explorable band of forest beyond the cabin door.
- Features: old woodpile, mark-tree with three-circle glyph, abandoned lantern post.
- Ecology: moss overgrowth appears in geometric bands around carved trees.
- Narrative role: introduces “pattern in nature” mystery.

### The Sleeper Paths (Hidden Routes)
- Narrow deer tracks only visible under specific light/mist conditions.
- Called “sleepy roads” by the younger daughter; map overlays shift subtly each night.
- Connects known spaces to older pre-logging footpaths and ritual clearings.
- Mechanical note: these paths should never unlock through a single item; they unlock through **observation + care actions**.

### Moonwell Hollow (Mid-Game Basin)
- Depression in the terrain where groundwater and moonlight pool in silvered film.
- Soundscape is unusually dampened; distant sounds arrive late.
- Ecological anomaly: fungi rings align with carved-symbol geometry.
- Potential puzzle type: route plotting by reflected symbol constellations.

### Windfall Orchard (Overgrown Homestead Ruin)
- Remains of an orchard swallowed by forest edge expansion.
- Old grafted fruit trees now intertwined with wild species.
- Holds family-history clues linking the mother’s research notes to present events.
- Emotional arc: grief and care can coexist; restoration vs extraction conflict.

### The Root Choir (Late-Game Bioluminescent Understory)
- Root caverns beneath a collapsed ridge; roots resonate at low frequencies.
- Bioluminescent lichen reacts to spoken phrases and footsteps.
- Core revelation zone: the forest “speaks” as distributed memory, not a monster.

## 2) Foundational Myths and Oral Fragments

### Myth A: The Three Sisters of Bark
- Folk tale says three guardian trees remember every promise made under their canopy.
- Their mark is rendered as three circles and a connecting line.
- Child version: “If you lie under the sisters, your shadow gets lost.”
- Adult interpretation: symbolic memory network maintained by ritual carvings.

### Myth B: The Borrowed Fire
- During hard winters, families took “borrowed fire” from the woods by carrying ember moss.
- Rule: what is borrowed must be returned through care (replanting, stream clearing, seed sharing).
- Narrative function: mirrors the game’s ecological reciprocity mechanic.

### Myth C: The Sleeping Road
- A path that “wakes only for those who walk gently and with purpose.”
- Historically dismissed as superstition, but journal records show repeatable sightings.
- Mechanically tied to low-impact behaviors and sequence-based environmental changes.

### Myth D: The Quiet Tax
- Old loggers said the forest always collected debt eventually: broken tools, spoiled harvests, vanished routes.
- In modern reading, this is ecosystem backlash to extraction pressure.
- This myth should be reframed from “curse” to “consequence.”

## 3) Timeline Beats (Story Backbone)

### Before Play (Past)
1. **Decades Ago** — Selective logging and stream diversion begin in Brindlewood.
2. **Years Ago** — Local families maintain informal stewardship customs; records fragment.
3. **Recent Past** — Mother researches pre-industrial route maps and oral stories.
4. **Inciting Loss** — Mother disappears during a mapping trip near Moonwell routes, presumed accident.
5. **Relocation** — Father and daughters move into the Hearthline Cabin to settle estate and investigate.

### Early Game
6. Notebook discovered; daughters independently reference identical three-circle motif.
7. Mark-tree symbol and notebook sketch confirmed as matching.
8. Fire restoration scene establishes home as operational base and emotional reset.

### Mid Game
9. Sleeper Paths intermittently appear; player correlates appearance with restorative actions.
10. Windfall Orchard reveals mother documented reciprocity rules, not hidden treasure routes.
11. Local myths decode into practical ecological guidance.

### Late Game
12. Root Choir confirms woods preserve “memory” through living systems and human ritual marks.
13. Family reconstructs mother’s final route; she staged protective diversions to prevent industrial access.
14. Resolution hinges on public truth + restoration plan, not conquest.

### End-State Themes
- The mystery is solved by attention, care, and shared trust.
- Family intimacy becomes a method of investigation, not just a subplot.
- Forest is partner and witness, not enemy.

## 4) Ecological Mystery Rules (Hard Canon)

1. **Reciprocity over Extraction**
   - Every resource action should have a restorative counterpart.
   - If player repeatedly takes without restoring, routes become noisier/less readable.

2. **Patterns Are Real, Not Supernatural Cheats**
   - Strange events must map to plausible ecological processes (water flow, fungal spread, root signaling) plus folklore framing.

3. **Observation Unlocks, Not Grinding**
   - Progress often requires noticing repeating motifs across dialogue, carvings, and terrain.

4. **Children Perceive First, Adults Verify Later**
   - Younger daughter gives metaphorical clues; older daughter proposes testable hypotheses.
   - Father mediates action by turning clues into practical steps.

5. **The Forest Responds to Behavior Over Time**
   - Kind/repair actions create subtle long-term visual and navigation shifts.
   - Aggressive/ignoring actions increase ambiguity and dead-end encounters.

6. **No Omniscient Villain**
   - Conflict comes from competing human intentions (care vs exploitation), not a singular evil entity.

7. **Warmth Is Mechanical and Emotional**
   - Hearth state should affect readability, morale dialogue, and clue synthesis scenes.

8. **Memory Is Distributed**
   - Clues are never fully contained in one artifact.
   - Real understanding emerges from combining notebook notes, child drawings, marks, and environmental evidence.

## 5) Key Character Anchors for Writing Consistency

- **Father (playable)**: practical, tired, quietly tender; learns to trust non-linear clues.
- **Older daughter (Clara)**: analytical, maps motifs, seeks proof.
- **Younger daughter (Mina)**: intuitive, poetic descriptions, pattern sensitivity.
- **Mother (absent/present through traces)**: field researcher balancing science and folklore respect.
- **Ranger Ivo (optional ally)**: skeptical at first, later supports restoration logistics.
- **Contractor Voss (human pressure)**: frames extraction as necessity; not cartoon villain.

Writing rule: even tension scenes should retain an undercurrent of care.

---

## 6) Reusable Motif Dictionary

- **Three circles + one line**: connected memory sites / route triad.
- **Weak fire → strong hearth**: trust and readiness.
- **Lantern light in fog**: provisional truth; enough to continue, not full certainty.
- **Moss bands on bark**: ecological “text” to be read.
- **Sleeping road**: systems that reveal themselves only under respectful interaction.

---

## 7) Content Guardrails (for future quests/dialogue)

- Avoid jump-scare horror framing; aim for gentle unease and wonder.
- Favor intimate scene transitions (tea, drying socks, shared notebook margin notes).
- Make every major discovery tie to both ecology and family relationship movement.
- Keep puzzle language readable and concrete; mystery should feel fair.
