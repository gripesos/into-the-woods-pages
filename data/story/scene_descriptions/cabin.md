# Cabin Scene Description

The cabin is small, practical, and lived-in by memory more than by comfort. Wood planks creak in familiar rhythms; a kettle ticks even when no one speaks. Dust catches warm light from a single west-facing window, while the back door frames the dark tree line like a second mouth to the story.

Key emotional tone: **intimate, fragile, stubbornly hopeful**.

## Atmosphere Notes
- Evening light shifts quickly, pushing the room from amber safety into blue uncertainty.
- Household objects feel inherited rather than chosen: repaired chair legs, mismatched mugs, a radio held together with tape.
- Silence is never empty; it carries distant wind, an occasional metallic hum, and unsaid family grief.

## Interactive Focus
- Table as clue hub (notebook, map fragments, cassette, marker rubbings).
- Hearth as comfort anchor and conversation reset space.
- Window and door thresholds as visual reminders of the forest pressing inward.

## Visual Style Cues (Retro Sierra + Ecological Mystery)
- Pixel-art readability first; silhouettes should remain clear at low resolution.
- Warm interior palette contrasted with cool exterior moonlight.
- Subtle animated loops: kettle steam, curtain drift, lamp flicker.
