# Into the Woods — Plot Outline

## High-Level Premise
A recently widowed father brings his two daughters to a family cabin at the edge of a protected forest, hoping to rebuild routine after loss. Instead, they uncover signs that the forest is being quietly altered by an illegal extraction project hidden beneath old folklore symbols. The family must decide whether to leave for safety or stay long enough to expose the truth before the ecosystem crosses a point of no return.

## Chapter / Act Structure

### Chapter 1 — The Quiet Cabin
- Arrival at the cabin; family tension is soft but present.
- The older daughter documents strange symbols and altered tree bark patterns.
- The younger daughter "hears" repeating shapes in wind and birdsong.
- Inciting mystery: a trail marker appears overnight, matching the girls' drawings.

### Chapter 2 — The Listening Forest
- Exploration expands to deeper trails, a ranger station, and a drained marsh edge.
- The family meets a retired ranger and a local mechanic hauling unregistered equipment.
- Evidence emerges: dead pollinator patches, hidden cables, and coded map symbols.
- Midpoint turn: the father realizes the symbol system links to old survey routes, not legends.

### Chapter 3 — Under the Roots
- They trace an underground pumping corridor disguised as restoration work.
- Emotional fracture: father wants to protect the girls by leaving; daughters insist on finishing what they started together.
- Climactic choice: sabotage exposure timing vs. immediate evacuation during storm conditions.
- Resolution: they reveal the operation to authorities and community allies, but the forest remains fragile—recovery is possible only with continued care.

## Mystery Thread (Ecological)
1. Repeating symbol "three circles + line" appears in drawings, tree scars, and survey stakes.
2. Wildlife behavior shifts around specific clearings and low-frequency machine hum.
3. Local paperwork claims restoration while physical evidence shows extraction and drainage.
4. Hidden infrastructure beneath roots connects to a private contractor route.
5. Final reveal: "folklore signs" were repurposed as covert navigation marks for illegal work.

## Emotional Thread (Family)
1. Father (Tomás) avoids discussing grief, over-indexing on logistics and safety.
2. Older daughter (Clara) seeks control through observation and proof.
3. Younger daughter (Lia) processes fear through imagination, becoming unexpectedly perceptive.
4. Their trust rebuilds as each perspective proves necessary: caution, analysis, intuition.
5. End state: not "fixed," but reconnected—a family choosing active care over retreat.
